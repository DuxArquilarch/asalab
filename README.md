Multi-Profile Aerodynamic Database & Dynamic InterpolationExpanded Airfoil Support: Integration of lookup systems and reference tables for NACA 4-digit and 5-digit airfoils (such as NACA 4412 and NACA 23012).Reynolds-Dependent Logarithmic Interpolation: Refinement of aerodynamic coefficient calculations ($a_0$, $\alpha_{L0}$, $C_{d0}$, $C_m$, $X_{Cp}$). Wing Planform Analysis (AsaT) & Silhouette RenderingSupported Wing Geometries: Analytical adaptation and application of physical corrections for three distinct planform geometries:Rectangular: Standard calculations based on empirical Oswald efficiency factors.Elliptical: Models the ideal lift distribution where induced drag is minimized ($e = 1.0$).Delta: Analytical approximation for low aspect ratio ($AR$) wings, blending linear lifting line theory with delta-specific sweep and slope corrections.Geometric Visualization Module (geo_visualizer.py): Development of a standalone graphical interface that reconstructs and plots airfoil physical coordinates (analytical thickness and mean camber lines) and the wing's semi-span silhouette in real time, allowing side-by-side or overlaid comparison of up to 3 profiles simultaneously.3. Advanced Aerodynamic Corrections & Real Atmosphere ModelingISA + Sutherland Atmospheric Model: Replacement of static constants with full International Standard Atmosphere (ISA) routines coupled with Sutherland's Law, dynamically calculating air viscosity, density, and speed of sound based on precise altitude and temperature inputs.Compressibility Effects: Implementation of the Prandtl-Glauert Correction ($1/\sqrt{1 - Ma^2}$) to adjust aerodynamic coefficients as the flow transitions into high-subsonic Mach regimes (up to Mach 0.7).Finite Wing Theory (LLT): Integration of Prandtl's Lifting Line Theory combined with Sadraey (2013) methodologies, converting 2D airfoil data into 3D wing coefficients ($C_{L\alpha}$) while factoring in aspect ratio ($AR$), stall curve recalculations, required weight to fly, and induced drag.4. Modernization of Graphics & Technical ReportsExpanded Aerodynamic Dashboard (2×3): Replacement of old, static heatmaps with high-definition interactive vector graphics generated via Plotly and saved locally. The new layout unifies essential performance curves into 6 quadrants:Lift Coefficient ($CL$ vs $\alpha$)Drag Coefficient ($CD$ vs $\alpha$)Aerodynamic Efficiency ($L/D$ vs $\alpha$)Pitching Moment Coefficient ($Cm$ vs $\alpha$)Center of Pressure Position ($XCp$ vs $\alpha$)Wing planform geometric silhouette.Reporting Module (aerodynamic_report.py): Automatic generation of a structured text document containing a comprehensive conceptual performance analysis of the simulated aircraft.5. Execution Flexibility (CLI & Autonomous GUI)Hot-Reload Monitoring: The main graphical interface (gui.py) now dynamically monitors the database.py file. Any manual changes, additions, or removals of airfoils are reflected instantly in the GUI without needing to restart the application.Unified Command Line Interface (CLI): Development of a dedicated terminal menu script (cli.py), enabling complete simulations, report building, and graphic exports directly through the terminal by passing the --cli or -c flags to the main asalab_Main.py script.
